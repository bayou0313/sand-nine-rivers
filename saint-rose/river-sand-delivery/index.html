Local Expertise

Saint Rose's geography is defined by its position on the natural levee of the Mississippi River — elevation drops quickly as you move away from River Road, and the soil transitions from silty loam to heavy clay within a few hundred feet. Water tables here are high year-round, typically 18 to 36 inches below surface, which means excavation work almost always encounters groundwater. River sand is the go-to material because it drains freely, resists compaction under saturated conditions, and won't hold water like the native clay-heavy soils that dominate this area.
